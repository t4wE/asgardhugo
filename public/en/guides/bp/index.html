Breakpoints [FBR / FHR / FCR / FRW / IAS]

In Diablo 2, a second is divided into 25 frames. As a result, every action (animation) is measured in these frames. By reaching certain breakpoints, you increase the speed of the animations.

Recommended breakpoints will be marked red.

It is worth mentioning that further suggestions are rather PvP-oriented, yet they are pretty similar to PvE ones.

FBR

FBR stands for Faster Block Rate. When you get hit with physical damage, you have a chance to block it (if you have a shield). During the block animation, other physical attacks don't deal any damage.

The maximum possible block is 75%. While running, the breakpoint is cut by 2/3 and becomes 25%. So, in most cases, it is better to walk and not to run. While walking the block is still 75%.

Assassin's unique skill Weapon Block only works when she is standing still or using WW. WB does not work while running or walking.

Block's animation length is different for every character. By reaching certain breakpoints, you'll be able to shorten this animation and, as a consequence, receive less damage.

(*) 1-handed swinging weapons include 1-handed axes, clubs, 1-handed maces, orbs, scepters, 1-handed swords, 2-handed swords wielded in one hand, throwing axes, and wands.

FHR starts at 1/16th hp loss of health for most skills (1/8 for Bone Spear, GA, and some others) and increases, the larger the percentage of HP you take down is as follows:

37.5% of the time, if it deals 1/16th of your max HP
75% of the time, if it deals 1/8th of your max HP
100% of the time, if it deals 1/4th of your max HP

For Bone Spear:

37.5% of the time, if it deals 1/8th of your max HP
75% of the time, if it deals 1/4th of your max HP
100% of the time, if it deals half of your max HP

After analyzing this, we can make several useful conclusions:

No Bugbelt:

  • In NvD at Spear dmg = 5900 - Druid should have >3980 hp to reduce the chance of getting FHR'd from 75% to 37.5% (8 sorb Spirit)
  • In NvB at Spear dmg = 5900 - Barb should have >8024 hp to reduce the chance of getting FHR'd from 37.5% to 0%
  • In DvB at Nado dmg = 5900 - Barb (40 fcr) with 46% DR should have >8656 hp to reduce the chance of getting FHR'd from 37.5% to 0%
  • In DvB at Nado dmg = 5900 - Barb (65 fcr) with 47% DR should have >8496 hp to reduce the chance of getting FHR'd from 37.5% to 0%

Bugbelt:

  • In NvD at Spear dmg = 6065 - Druid should have >4092 hp to reduce the chance of getting FHR'd from 75% to 37.5% (8 sorb Spirit)
  • In NvB at Spear dmg = 6065 - Barb should have >8248 hp to reduce the chance of getting FHR'd from 37.5% to 0%
  • In DvB at Nado dmg = 6086 - Barb (40/65 fcr) with 50% DR should have >8272 hp to reduce the chance of getting FHR'd from 37.5% to 0%
